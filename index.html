Basics of SciComm

An introduction to Science Communication

Science Communication (often shortened to SciComm) is the practice of making scientific knowledge accessible, engaging, and relevant to diverse audiences. It spans journalism, museums, education, policy, and digital media, and plays a crucial role in connecting research with society.

At university level, SciComm is studied both as a set of professional skills—writing, presenting, designing exhibits—and as a field of research that examines how people understand, trust, and use science in everyday life. This site introduces core concepts and methods to help students and practitioners navigate the landscape of modern science communication.

Key Methodologies in SciComm

SciComm relies on a combination of approaches that balance accuracy with accessibility:

  • Framing: Presenting scientific issues in ways that connect to social values and everyday concerns.
  • Narrative: Using storytelling to make complex research meaningful and memorable.
  • Engagement: Creating opportunities for dialogue, participation, and co‑creation rather than one‑way transmission.
  • Visualization: Employing graphics, models, and interactive media to make abstract concepts tangible.
  • Evaluation: Measuring impact through surveys, interviews, analytics, and long‑term follow‑ups.

Together, these methodologies help communicators translate evidence into experiences that foster curiosity, trust, and informed decision‑making.
