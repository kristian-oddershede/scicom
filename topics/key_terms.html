Basics of SciCom - Key Terms

Why key terms matter

Shared language helps teams design, evaluate, and improve science communication. These ten concise terms capture the core ideas practitioners use to align goals, audiences, methods, and ethics.

Main thing to know:
Clear concepts = clearer choices—these terms guide what you communicate, how you design, and how you measure impact.

Ten central terms at a glance

Term Core idea
Publics Different groups with distinct contexts and needs—not a single “general public.”
Engagement Mutual interaction where audiences participate, question, and contribute—not one‑way messaging.
Trust Confidence built through transparency, evidence, and respect for uncertainty and limits.
Framing How issues are presented to shape meaning, relevance, and decisions without distorting facts.
Narrative Structured stories that connect evidence to lived experience and motivate understanding.
Inquiry Hands‑on observation, testing, and reflection that turn curiosity into learning.
Expertise Knowledge plus accountability; recognizing scientific, local, and experiential expertise.
Uncertainty What is unknown or provisional; communicating limits and methods for reducing them.
Inclusion Access, representation, and agency so diverse communities shape content and practice.
Evaluation Evidence of impact using mixed methods to assess engagement, learning, equity, and trust.
